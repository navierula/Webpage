My name is Navie Narula and I am a graduate student at Columbia University studying computer science with a special focus on natural language processing and computational journalism. Prior to that, I completed an undergraduate degree in both computer science and secondary English education at Boston University.

I am interested in text mining, language extraction, and pattern recognition as it may pertain to any type of unstructured data. I hope to use my computation skills for social good, especially when it comes to uncovering meaning in language. This spring, I computationally examined language in subreddits comments related to eating disorders. I also built a tool to detect emotion in text, which would then generate music based on that emotion label. In the past, I've analyzed trending topics on Twitter based on sentiment derived from a self-devised scoring algorithm.

As a result of taking a class on computational models for speech, I have become interested in audio input in addition to text. My thesis project will likely focus on speech recognition and emotion capture in AI in some way.

I am currently looking for full-time jobs and will graduate in May 2018. This summer, I am a software engineering intern at Quid. I have also previously worked at the MIT Press, Cambridge Community Television, and the BU Hariri Institute for Computing. Check out some of the things I've built below and please reach out to me if you would like to collaborate on a project, or simply find me at your next hackathon!
